## Overview

This project explores a **salt-based additive** designed to modify the sweetness profile of **aspartame** in beverages.  
The additive is **not a sweetener**; instead, it acts as a **taste-modulating component** that adjusts how aspartame is perceived.

The goal is to make aspartame-based drinks feel closer to **sucrose/HFCS** by reducing lingering sweetness, improving mouthfeel, and minimizing metallic or diet-like off-notes.

This README outlines the conceptual R&D structure, human workflow, and scientific rationale behind the additive.  
It does **not** include manufacturing instructions, temperatures, timings, or quantities.

---

## 1. R&D Goal

### Objective
Design a salt-based additive that, when added to aspartame, produces:

- Less lingering sweetness  
- More “sugar-like” mouthfeel  
- No salty, metallic, or bitter off-notes  

### Positioning
- Not a sweetener  
- A modular taste-shaping component for diet beverages and low-calorie products  

---

## 2. Scientific Backbone

### Core Idea
Use mineral ions as **entourage molecules** around aspartame to:

- Influence diffusion through tongue mucus  
- Modulate sweet and calcium-sensing receptors  
- Adjust onset, peak, and linger of sweetness perception  

### Key Constraint
- **NaCl alone is insufficient**  
- Effective systems use **K/Mg/Ca salts** at controlled levels  
- NaCl may appear only as a trace balancing component

## 3. Kitchen-Style R&D Environment

A human-operated R&D space modeled after a kitchen, but following scientific controls.

### Core Modules

#### Module A — Water & Base Handling
Kitchen analogue: kettles, carafes, filters  
R&D role: prepare purified water with controlled pH and minimal background minerals.

#### Module B — Mineral Stock Preparation
Kitchen analogue: measuring cups, bowls, stirrers  
R&D role: prepare food-grade mineral stock solutions at known concentrations.

#### Module C — Mixing & Conditioning
Kitchen analogue: stand mixer, immersion blender, temperature-controlled bath  
R&D role: combine mineral stocks with aspartame solutions and allow them to equilibrate.

#### Module D — Sensory Testing Station
Kitchen analogue: tasting counter  
R&D role: compare HFCS cola, plain aspartame cola, and additive prototypes.

#### Module E — Iteration & Logging
Kitchen analogue: recipe notebook  
R&D role: track mineral ratios, sensory outcomes, off-notes, and promising prototypes.

---

## 4. Human Process Flow (Conceptual)

### Step 1 — Define Targets
- Select a reference drink (e.g., HFCS cola).  
- Describe desired sweetness curve and mouthfeel.

### Step 2 — Build Prototypes
- Prepare multiple aspartame-based test beverages.  
- Apply varied mineral profiles within safe, food-grade ranges.

### Step 3 — Sensory Panels
- Blind, randomized tasting.  
- Score sweetness intensity, onset, linger, mouthfeel, and off-notes.

### Step 4 — Narrow & Refine
- Identify 2–3 promising mineral profiles.  
- Iterate around those profiles.

### Step 5 — Pre-Industrial Handoff
Provide labs with:
- Target mineral ratios  
- Sensory data  
- Intended beverage use-case  

Labs handle:
- Safety/toxicology  
- Regulatory review  
- Industrial process design  

---

## 5. Scaling Through Kitchen-Style Units

A larger factory can be built from multiple “kitchen-like” nodes:

- **Module A/B/C stations** acting as smart preparation units  
- **Central sensory & QA hub** for human validation  
- **Industrial backbone** ensuring:
  - Precise dosing  
  - Purity checks  
  - Batch tracking  
  - Compliance
